Biography

One of the foremost conductors of his generation, Solti Foundation award winner Christopher Hill has appeared as a conductor throughout the United States, Germany, Austria, Denmark, Greece, and Spain. He has been distinguished through invitiations to participate in the most prestigious international competitions for the art, including the Nikolai Malko, Dimitris Mitropoulos and Cadaqués Conducting Competitions chaired by Lorin Maazel, Claus Peter Flor, and Gennady Rozhdestvensky respectively. His most recent engagements include a televised appearance with the Danish National Radio Orchestra, the MIT Symphony and the Boston Opera Collaborative.

Mr. Hill is the former music director of both the Portsmouth Symphony Orchestra and the Director of Orchestras at the University of New Hampshire. He has served on the conducting staff of the University of Michigan and of the Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music. Mr. Hill has worked with Ulrich Nicolai, Kurt Masur, Bruno Weil, Marin Alsop, Leonard Slatkin, Paavo Järvi, Hugh Wolff and Mark Gibson.

An uncompromising advocate for artistic quality and improved access to arts education, Mr. Hill is an enthusiastic cultural and artistic ambassador for the communities he represents. His current projects aim to bridge the gap between artistic excellence, arts education and fiscal sustenance using technology as a catalyst.

Mr. Hill’s most recent research and intellectual work explores the relationship of visual art, architecture and music. His doctoral thesis, The Intersection of the Arts in Paris and Vienna: Art Nouveau and the Symphony during the Fin-de-Siècle (2012) and lecture performance, Fünf Orchesterstücke (Five Pieces for Orchestra): A Comparison of Performing Editions as Viewed through the Formal Aesthetics of Adolf Loos (2012) both received high accolades.

Mr. Hill was awarded the prestigious Doctor of Musical Arts degree from the Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music in 2012. He is an avid bicyclist and runner, and enjoys combining his love for science with art as a software engineer.
